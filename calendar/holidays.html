Philippine Regular Holidays 2023

Back to Calendar

New Year's Day — January 1 (Sun)

New Year’s Day marks the beginning of the calendar year and is widely celebrated throughout the Philippines. It is a time filled with hope, new resolutions, and festive gatherings as families and communities welcome a fresh start.

Maundy Thursday — April 6

Maundy Thursday is a significant Christian observance commemorating the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with his disciples. Filipinos reflect on themes of humility, service, and sacrifice on this holy day.

Good Friday — April 7

Good Friday marks the crucifixion and death of Jesus Christ. It is a solemn day of mourning and reflection, observed with religious ceremonies and processions throughout the country.

Araw ng Kagitingan — April 10 (Mon)

Known as the Day of Valor, Araw ng Kagitingan honors the bravery and sacrifices of Filipino and American soldiers during World War II. This solemn day reminds the nation of courage, patriotism, and the importance of freedom.

Eid'l Fitr — April 21 (Fri)

Eid’l Fitr is a major Islamic holiday celebrating the end of Ramadan, the month of fasting. It is a joyful occasion marked by prayer, feasting, and community gatherings among Filipino Muslims.

Labor Day — May 1 (Mon)

Labor Day honors the hard work and contributions of Filipino workers across all sectors. It is a day to recognize labor rights, achievements, and the ongoing struggle for fair working conditions.

Independence Day — June 12 (Mon)

Independence Day commemorates the Philippines’ declaration of independence from Spanish colonial rule in 1898. It is a day of national pride, patriotic celebrations, and remembrance of the country’s fight for freedom.

Eid'l Adha — June 28 (Wed)

Eid’l Adha, or the Feast of Sacrifice, is an important Islamic festival commemorating Ibrahim’s willingness to sacrifice his son as an act of obedience to God. It is observed with prayers, charity, and community gatherings.

National Heroes Day — August 28 (Mon)

National Heroes Day honors all Filipino heroes who have sacrificed their lives for the country’s liberty and justice. It inspires patriotism and serves as a reminder of the enduring spirit of the Filipino people.

Bonifacio Day — November 27 (Mon)

Bonifacio Day commemorates Andrés Bonifacio, one of the country’s revolutionary leaders who played a key role in the fight against Spanish colonization. It is a celebration of courage and nationalism.

Christmas Day — December 25 (Mon)

Christmas Day celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ and is one of the most widely observed holidays in the Philippines. It highlights the country’s strong Christian faith, family unity, and festive traditions.

Rizal Day — December 30 (Sat)

Rizal Day honors José Rizal, a national hero whose writings and martyrdom greatly influenced the Philippine revolution. The day commemorates his legacy and the enduring fight for justice and independence.
